// THE FIELD KIT

The tools inside PaleoDex.

A real-world fossil map, a 74,000-species catalogue, an excavation game, a paleogeographic timeline, and a live science news feed.

RADAR

Find fossils near you

The Radar uses your location to show which prehistoric species have been found near you — with distance and direction. Navigate to any country or continent to see what's been discovered there. Backed by 1.8 million real fossil occurrence records.

FOSSIL-DEX

74,000+ real species, properly sourced

Every genus has a sourced field dossier: real photo, scientific classification from the Open Tree of Life, age, formation, locality, diet, and rarity tier. Nothing invented.

FOSSILRUN

Dig it out

Time your strikes in the excavation mini-game. Clean timing means a better-condition specimen — every find is a real, photographed genus.

SIGNAL

Live from the dig

A bilingual paleontology news stream — new species and fresh fossils from major science outlets, in English and Spanish.

COLLECTION RANK

Climb the ranks

An 8-tier ladder scored on collection size, perfect specimens, field finds, and rarity — with "next move" tips to guide your progress.

// HOW IT WORKS

Explore, hunt, and collect.

01

Explore

Open the Radar to see real fossil discovery sites near your location, or browse the map to any region of the world. Every pin is a documented occurrence from the fossil record — species, age, and coordinates included.

02

Hunt

In FossilRun the game radar is always active, tracking dig sites in real time as you move. When you reach a site, time your strikes on the excavation ring to uncover the specimen. Every species revealed is a real, documented genus.

03

Collect

Excavated species go into your Museum, ranked by rarity and condition. Look up any of the 74,000+ genera in the Fossil-DEX — the full sourced catalogue — for its scientific classification, fossil record, and field data.

// THE DATA

No invented creatures. No made-up stats.

PaleoDex is built on open paleontology data — the Paleobiology Database, GBIF, the Open Tree of Life and more. If a fact isn't sourced, PaleoDex leaves it blank rather than guessing. That's the rule, and it's genuinely true.

SOURCE WHAT IT PROVIDES
Paleobiology Database (PBDB)The backbone: genera, ~1.8M fossil occurrences, ages, formations, environments, and paleo-coordinates.
GBIFExtinct-vs-living status flags across thousands of genera.
Wikipedia / Wikimedia CommonsField write-ups and real species photographs.
WikidataStructured facts and cross-references.
Open Tree of LifeThe live scientific classification (Kingdom → Genus lineage) shown in every dossier.
PhyloPicClean CC0 category silhouettes used as image fallbacks.
Encyclopedia of Life (EOL)Supplementary trait and ecology enrichment.
GPlates / paleogeographic modelThe continental-drift reconstructions on the Continental Drift map.
MapTiler · MapLibre · OpenStreetMapThe live base world map.
